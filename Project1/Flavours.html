FLAVOURS




Strawberry

Strawberry ice cream is a classic, refreshing frozen dessert made by blending fresh strawberries
or fruit puree into an ice cream base of cream, sugar, and milk. Known for its light pink color and
creamy-sweet profile, it is one of the oldest recorded ice cream flavors in history.

Vanilla

Vanilla ice cream is the most popular and classic flavor globally. It is traditionally made from
a simple base of cream, milk, sugar, and natural vanilla flavoring (either real vanilla beans, vanilla extract,
or artificial vanillin). Because of its subtle, sweet flavor, it acts as a staple dessert.

Butterscotch

Butterscotch ice cream is a rich, creamy dessert flavored withbutterscotch—a confection made from butter and brown sugar.
It features a sweet, toffee-like base and is famous for its crunchy golden "praline" bits, which are made by caramelizing sugar
and tossing in chopped nuts (like cashews or almonds).

Chocolate

Chocolate ice cream is a frozen dessert typically made by blending cocoa powder with cream, milk, sugar, and egg yolks.
Predating vanilla, it originated in 17th-century Italy and is now one of the most popular ice cream flavors worldwide.
